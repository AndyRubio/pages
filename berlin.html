4-Day Berlin Itinerary

Day 1 — Orientation & Classic Berlin

  • Morning: Walk Unter den Linden to Brandenburg Gate. Good first sweep of the city’s historic core.
  • Lunch: Grab something quick around Pariser Platz or head to Potsdamer Platz.
  • Afternoon: Visit the Topography of Terror museum. Excellent, heavy, but essential.
  • Late Afternoon: Explore the Sony Center & Filmhaus — Fassbinder exhibits appear periodically; worth checking for temporary installations.
  • Evening (before 10pm): Dinner at a modern German spot in Mitte; early walk along the Spree near the Reichstag.

Day 2 — Museums & Fassbinder Connections

  • Morning: Museum Island — focus on the Neues Museum (Egyptian collection) or the Alte Nationalgalerie for German Romanticism.
  • Lunch: Hackescher Markt area — lots of cafés.
  • Afternoon: Visit the Deutsche Kinemathek at Potsdamer Platz. Its Fassbinder materials are the best in the city.
  • Late Afternoon: Explore the Hackesche Höfe courtyard system — art studios, small galleries.
  • Evening (before 10pm): Early screening at Arsenal Kino (they often show Fassbinder). If nothing relevant is on, pick any repertory screening.

Day 3 — Art, Techno History & Kraftwerk Landmarks

  • Morning: Hamburger Bahnhof — Berlin’s top contemporary art museum. Big industrial space, good for solo wandering.
  • Lunch: Café inside the museum or nearby Europacity.
  • Afternoon: Walk to the former Kraftwerk power plant (home of Tresor). Even if not clubbing, the architecture is pure industrial Berlin.
  • Late Afternoon: Visit Tresor’s exhibition space if open — they sometimes run small techno-culture retrospectives.
  • Evening (before 10pm): Grab dinner around Friedrichshain; stroll the East Side Gallery before it gets dark.

Day 4 — Charlottenburg & Quiet Culture

  • Morning: Charlottenburg Palace gardens — peaceful, easy stroll.
  • Lunch: Kantstraße — strong Asian food options.
  • Afternoon: Sammlung Scharf-Gerstenberg or Berggruen Museum — compact but excellent collections (surrealism/modernism).
  • Late Afternoon: Kaffee und Kuchen in a traditional Berlin café.
  • Evening (before 10pm): Wind down with a river cruise if weather allows; otherwise, Kurfürstendamm bookstores and early dinner.
Enjoy the trip — Berlin rewards curiosity, even without staying out all night.
