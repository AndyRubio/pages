Chord Runs Up and Down the Keyboard (Grade 1 Level)

Focus: simple shapes, repeatable patterns, and movement you can actually control on your UDO Super Gemini.

1. Start With Two Easy Chord Types

At Grade 1, don’t mess around with fancy 7ths yet. Get comfortable with:

  • Major and minor triads
    Example in C: C–E–G (C major), A–C–E (A minor)
  • Sus2 chords (super friendly for runs)
    Example in C: C–D–G (Csus2)

These give you clean harmony without needing advanced theory.

2. Use Chord Shapes That Travel Easily

Triad Inversions

Example with C major:

  • Root position: C–E–G
  • 1st inversion: E–G–C
  • 2nd inversion: G–C–E

Sus2 Shape (The “Glide”)

Csus2: C–D–G
Dsus2: D–E–A
Esus2: E–F♯–B

3. The Triad Inversion Ladder

  1. Play C–E–G (root)
  2. Play E–G–C (1st inv)
  3. Play G–C–E (2nd inv)
  4. Jump to the next C higher and repeat

RH FINGERING:

Root: 1–3–5 | 1st: 1–2–5 | 2nd: 1–3–5

4. Parallel Sus2 Drift

  1. Play Csus2: C–D–G
  2. Slide up one white key: Dsus2 (D–E–A)
  3. Continue up the "white key" scale

Use 1–2–5 for every single chord.

5. Two-Hand Anchor

Left Hand: Hold long bass notes (C... D... E...)
Right Hand: Perform your chosen run over the top.

6. Daily Practice (5 Mins)

Slow and clean is the goal.

Min 1-2: Triad Ladder
Min 3-4: Sus2 Drift
Min 5: Combine hands
