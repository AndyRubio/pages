Chord Runs Up and Down the Keyboard (Grade 1 Level)

Focus: simple shapes, repeatable patterns, and movement you can actually control on your UDO Super Gemini.

1. Start With Two Easy Chord Types

At Grade 1, don’t mess around with fancy 7ths yet. Get comfortable with:

  • Major and minor triads
    Example in C: C–E–G (C major), A–C–E (A minor)
  • Sus2 chords (super friendly for runs)
    Example in C: C–D–G (Csus2)

These give you clean harmony without needing advanced theory.

2. Use Chord Shapes That Travel Easily

Triad Inversions (One Chord, Three Shapes)

Example with C major:

  • Root position: C–E–G
  • 1st inversion: E–G–C
  • 2nd inversion: G–C–E

These let you move up the keyboard with small, controlled shifts.

Sus2 Shape (The “Glide” Shape)

Csus2: C–D–G
Dsus2: D–E–A
Esus2: E–F♯–B

Your hand barely changes shape. This is perfect for fast, smooth chord runs.

3. First Run: The Triad Inversion Ladder (Beginner-Friendly)

Do this with C major to start:

  1. Play C–E–G (root position)
  2. Play E–G–C (1st inversion)
  3. Play G–C–E (2nd inversion)
  4. Jump to the next C higher on the keyboard and repeat the 3 shapes
  5. Come back down: 2nd → 1st → root → next lower root, etc.

Suggested Right-Hand Fingering

  • Root position (C–E–G): 1–3–5
  • 1st inversion (E–G–C): 1–2–5
  • 2nd inversion (G–C–E): 1–3–5 (or 1–2–4 if that’s easier)

Keep the fingering the same every time. Consistency builds speed.

4. Second Run: Parallel Sus2 Drift (Very Easy, Sounds Polished)

This one is stupidly simple and works great on a lush synth pad.

Step-by-step

  1. Play Csus2: C–D–G
  2. Move everything up one white key to D: D–E–A (Dsus2)
  3. Then Esus2, Fsus2, Gsus2, etc.
  4. Come back down the same way.

Your hand shape is almost identical each time, so you can move faster without losing control.

Suggested Fingering for Sus2

Use 1–2–5 (thumb–index–little finger) for all sus2 chords in the right hand.

5. Two-Hand Trick: Make Runs Sound Huge With Simple Notes

You don’t need advanced left-hand skills. Just anchor the harmony.

  • Left hand: play single bass notes
    Example: C → D → E → F → G (whole notes or held notes)
  • Right hand: play the triad ladder or sus2 drift over those notes.

On a synth, this immediately sounds bigger and more musical, even if the right hand is very simple.

6. A 5-Minute Daily Routine (Realistic for Grade 1)

Do this every day for 5 minutes. Don’t rush; slow and clean is the goal.

  1. Triad Ladder in C
    Right hand only. Root → 1st → 2nd → next root up, then back down.
  2. Sus2 Drift
    Csus2 → Dsus2 → Esus2 → Fsus2 → Gsus2 and back down.
  3. With Left Hand
    Left hand plays C–D–E–F–G as long notes. Right hand does either the triad ladder or the sus2 drift on top.

After a couple of weeks of this, running chords up and down the keyboard will feel much more natural, and your Super Gemini will actually show it off instead of exposing every hesitation.
