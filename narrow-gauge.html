7-Day Narrow-Gauge Rail Focus · Switzerland

Autumn Rail Itineraries for Switzerland Narrow-Gauge Lines

Two 7-day options: one hardcore Rhätische Bahn + Matterhorn Gotthard Bahn focus, one “grand circuit” adding Zentralbahn, Bernese Oberland, and GoldenPass. You can mix and match days between them.

Trip length: 7 days
Season: Next autumn (Sept–Nov)
Focus: Metre-gauge & rack railways
Base hubs: Chur · St. Moritz · Andermatt · Zermatt · Lucerne · Interlaken · Montreux

Planning Notes

  • Use SBB app / website for exact 2026 times; patterns here follow typical current timetables.
  • A Swiss Travel Pass or Half-Fare card is almost mandatory for this much train travel; mountain lines like Gornergrat/Rochers-de-Naye may only be discounted, not free.
  • Many “panorama” trains (Bernina Express, Glacier Express, GoldenPass Express) require or benefit from seat reservations.
  • Narrow-gauge highlights you’re targeting: RhB (Albula, Bernina, Arosa), MGB (Furka/Glacier Express route, Zermatt), Zentralbahn Brünig line, Bernese Oberland Bahn, MOB GoldenPass, and the rack lines (Gornergrat, Rochers-de-Naye).
  • Expect short daylight and possible early snow at higher altitude by late autumn; check for maintenance closures, especially on mountain lines.

Pace & Hubs

  • Don’t move hotels every night unless you enjoy it. Typical hub pattern: Chur → St. Moritz → Andermatt → Zermatt (Itinerary A) or Lucerne → Interlaken → Montreux → Zermatt (Itinerary B).
  • All days assume arrival via Zurich. If you fly into Geneva, shift the start/end accordingly (easier for Montreux & Valais).
  • “Must-ride” lines are called out per day; if you need to cut anything, cut local sightseeing before you cut those.
Itinerary A – Hardcore Narrow-Gauge (RhB + MGB)

Heavy focus on Rhätische Bahn and Matterhorn Gotthard Bahn, including Albula, Bernina, Glacier Express route and Gornergrat. Very rail-fan biased; minimal fluff.

Rail-Fan Intensive
Zurich → Chur → St. Moritz → Andermatt → Zermatt → Montreux/Zurich Core lines: RhB · MGB · Gornergrat Bahn
Day 1 Zurich → Chur → Arosa → Chur
RhB Arosa line

Theme: Get onto metre-gauge immediately and warm up with a classic branch line.

Outline

  • Arrive Zurich → train to Chur (standard-gauge IC/IR).
  • Afternoon return trip Chur ↔ Arosa (RhB metre-gauge; steep, curvy, scenic).
  • Evening in Chur; overnight Chur.

Must-ride

Chur–Arosa RhB line (metre-gauge mountain branch).

Day 2 Chur → Filisur → St. Moritz
Albula line

Theme: UNESCO-listed Albula route with the Landwasser Viaduct.

Outline

  • Morning RhB regional Chur → Filisur via Albula.
  • Photo stop and viewpoint for Landwasser Viaduct (short walk from Filisur).
  • Continue Filisur → St. Moritz (Albula summit tunnels and spirals).
  • Optional local hops: St. Moritz ↔ Pontresina/Samedan; overnight St. Moritz or Pontresina.

Must-ride

Albula Railway (Chur–Filisur–St. Moritz, RhB metre-gauge).

Day 3 St. Moritz → Tirano → St. Moritz
Bernina line

Theme: Full Bernina day – glaciers, high pass, Italy and back.

Outline

  • Morning: regional or Bernina Express St. Moritz → Tirano.
  • Stops en route if you want: Ospizio Bernina, Alp Grüm, Poschiavo.
  • Lunch and short wander in Tirano.
  • Afternoon return Tirano → St. Moritz/Pontresina.

Must-ride

Full Bernina line (St. Moritz–Ospizio Bernina–Alp Grüm–Poschiavo–Tirano).

Day 4 St. Moritz → Chur → Disentis → Andermatt
Glacier route

Theme: Glacier Express route without committing to the through tourist train.

Outline

  • Morning: RhB St. Moritz → Chur (Albula again, different direction).
  • Continue Chur → Disentis/Mustér (RhB, metre-gauge).
  • Change to MGB: Disentis → Andermatt (rack sections, Furka Oberalp line).
  • Overnight in Andermatt.

Must-ride

RhB Disentis–Chur + MGB Disentis–Andermatt (key Glacier Express segments).

Day 5 Andermatt → Brig → Zermatt
Furka & Mattertal

Theme: Finish the Glacier Express route and climb to Zermatt.

Outline

  • Morning: MGB Andermatt → Brig via Furka base tunnel.
  • Coffee break and quick look at Brig station.
  • Midday: Brig → Zermatt (MGB; continuous metre-gauge up the Mattertal).
  • Settle in and overnight Zermatt.

Must-ride

Entire MGB Andermatt–Brig–Zermatt stretch.

Day 6 Zermatt & Gornergrat
Rack railway

Theme: Focus on pure rack railway and Matterhorn views.

Outline

  • Morning: ride Gornergrat Bahn (Zermatt–Gornergrat, 1000 mm rack) up and back.
  • Optional: second ride later for different light conditions.
  • Extra for nerds: bash the Zermatt–Täsch MGB shuttle once or twice.
  • Overnight again in Zermatt.

Must-ride

Gornergrat Railway (rack, high altitude, iconic views).

Day 7 Zermatt → Visp/Brig → Montreux or Zurich
Exit day

Theme: Final narrow-gauge stretch, then standard-gauge out.

Outline

  • Morning: MGB Zermatt → Visp or Brig.
  • Option 1: IC to Zurich for flight/home.
  • Option 2: head toward Montreux if you want to bolt on GoldenPass days from Itinerary B.

Must-ride

Last descent on MGB from Zermatt.

Itinerary B – Grand Narrow-Gauge Circuit

Adds Zentralbahn’s Brünig line, Bernese Oberland Bahn and the MOB GoldenPass route to the mix, with a slightly more balanced “rail + scenery” feel.

Balanced Circuit
Zurich → Lucerne → Interlaken → Montreux → Zermatt → Zurich Core lines: Zentralbahn · BOB · MOB · MGB · Gornergrat
Day 1 Zurich → Lucerne → Interlaken
Brünig line

Theme: Straight onto metre-gauge via lakes and the Brünig Pass.

Outline

  • Morning/early afternoon: Zurich → Lucerne (standard-gauge, short ride).
  • Then Lucerne → Interlaken Ost (Zentralbahn Brünig line; metre-gauge with rack sections).
  • Check in and wander Interlaken; overnight Interlaken.

Must-ride

Zentralbahn Brünig line (Lucerne–Interlaken).

Day 2 Interlaken ↔ Lauterbrunnen & Grindelwald
BOB branches

Theme: Bernese Oberland twin valleys on metre-gauge BOB.

Outline

  • Morning: Interlaken Ost → Lauterbrunnen (BOB branch).
  • Short walk in Lauterbrunnen; back to Zweilütschinen.
  • Then Zweilütschinen → Grindelwald on the other BOB branch.
  • Afternoon in Grindelwald or return to Interlaken; overnight Interlaken or Grindelwald.

Must-ride

Both BOB branches: Interlaken–Lauterbrunnen and Interlaken–Grindelwald.

Day 3 Interlaken → Zweisimmen → Montreux
GoldenPass

Theme: Full GoldenPass line including the MOB narrow-gauge section.

Outline

  • Take GoldenPass Express or regional services Interlaken Ost → Zweisimmen and onward Zweisimmen → Montreux.
  • Enjoy gauge-changing train (on the Express) and lake/vineyard scenery approaching Montreux.
  • Overnight Montreux.

Must-ride

MOB narrow-gauge between Zweisimmen and Montreux.

Day 4 Montreux → Rochers-de-Naye → Montreux
Rack line

Theme: Another rack railway, this time above Lake Geneva.

Outline

  • Rack railway Montreux → Rochers-de-Naye and back (MVR, metre-gauge rack).
  • Afternoon at leisure in Montreux/Vevey; optional short MOB local to Montbovon and back.
  • Overnight Montreux.

Must-ride

Montreux–Glion–Rochers-de-Naye rack railway.

Day 5 Montreux → Visp → Zermatt
MGB ascent

Theme: Move to Valais and climb to car-free Zermatt.

Outline

  • Standard-gauge IC Montreux → Visp.
  • Metre-gauge MGB Visp → Zermatt (Mattertal ascent).
  • Explore village; overnight Zermatt.

Must-ride

Visp–Zermatt MGB line.

Day 6 Zermatt & Gornergrat
Rack + views

Theme: Same core as Itinerary A Day 6 – you can’t really skip this.

Outline

  • Ride Gornergrat Bahn at least once.
  • Optionally bash Zermatt–Täsch shuttles for extra rolling stock spotting.
  • Overnight Zermatt.

Must-ride

Gornergrat Railway (again, because it’s that good).

Day 7 Zermatt → Brig/Visp → Zurich
Return

Theme: Final narrow-gauge descent then standard-gauge back to your airport.

Outline

  • Morning: MGB Zermatt → Visp or Brig.
  • Standard-gauge IC/IR via Lötschberg or via Bern back to Zurich.
  • Fly out or overnight near Zurich Airport if you have an early flight.

Must-ride

One last MGB narrow-gauge run from Zermatt.
