Optimal strategy: staggered parallel learning

Phase 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–2)

Goal: Decode all three; build mental maps.

  • Learn all three simultaneously, but only to ~50–60% tempo.
  • Priorities:
    • Fingering fixed
    • Rhythm exact
    • Hands separately where needed
  • Time split (example):
    • Piece A (hardest): 40%
    • Piece B: 35%
    • Piece C (easiest): 25%

Why: early exposure prevents later panic and allows subconscious consolidation.

Phase 2 — Deepening (Weeks 3–5)

Goal: One piece becomes exam-secure while others mature.

  • Choose one anchor piece (usually the hardest or most technical).
  • That piece reaches:
    • Full tempo
    • Reliable memory
    • Musical shaping
  • The other two:
    • Stay under tempo
    • Focus on accuracy and consistency

Daily rule:

  • Never practice only one piece in a day
  • Always rotate order to avoid dependency effects

Phase 3 — Integration (Weeks 6–7)

Goal: All three are performance-ready.

  • All pieces now at or above exam tempo
  • Begin:
    • Full run-throughs
    • Random starting points
    • Deliberate mistake recovery
  • Practice transitions between pieces as if in the exam (no reset mindset)

Phase 4 — Exam conditioning (Final 10–14 days)

Goal: Reliability under pressure.

  • Daily mock exams:
    • Sit down
    • Play all three once
    • No stopping
  • Post-run diagnosis only
  • Light technical maintenance only; no heavy rebuilding
