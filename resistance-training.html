Gym Resistance Training Plan

Goal: build strength and function safely with progressive, joint-friendly training.

2–3 sessions/week Full-body sessions Machines first Stop 2–3 reps before failure Progress slowly

Core Principles

  • Frequency beats intensity: 2–3 sessions/week; avoid training the same muscles hard on consecutive days.
  • Machines > free weights (initially): lower balance demand; easier setup; lower injury risk.
  • Controlled reps: ~2–3 seconds up, ~3 seconds down; avoid momentum.
  • Leave reps in reserve: stop with ~2–3 reps “left in the tank”.
  • Progress gradually: add weight or reps (not both at once).

Weekly Structure

  • Option A (start here): 2 full-body sessions/week (e.g., Mon & Thu)
  • Option B (after 6–8 weeks): 3 full-body sessions/week (e.g., Mon/Wed/Fri)
  • Session length: ~45–60 minutes

Progression Rules

  • Start lighter than you think.
  • When you hit the top of the rep range on all sets, increase weight next time.
  • Typical jump on machines: 2.5–5 lb (1–2 kg).
Track sets/reps/weight each session. Consistency + small increases drive results.
Red flags (stop/modify): sharp joint pain, dizziness, chest discomfort, or pain lasting >48 hours after training.

Session Template (Full-Body)

Warm-Up (10 minutes)

  1. 5 minutes light cardio (treadmill walk, bike, or elliptical)
  2. Dynamic prep: arm circles × 10, hip circles × 10, sit-to-stand × 10, shoulder shrugs × 10
Avoid long static stretching before lifting. Keep everything pain-free.

Lower Body (priority: mobility + strength)

1) Leg Press (machine) 2–3 sets × 8–12 reps

Cue: feet shoulder-width; knees track over toes; controlled depth.

2) Seated Leg Curl 2 sets × 10–12 reps

Cue: smooth reps; no hip lifting off the pad.

3) Leg Extension (light) 1–2 sets × 10–12 reps

Cue: stop if knee pain; keep range comfortable.

4) Calf Raise (machine or bodyweight) 2 sets × 12–15 reps

Cue: full control; pause briefly at the top.

Upper Body Push (chest, shoulders, triceps)

5) Chest Press (machine) 2–3 sets × 8–12 reps

Cue: seat height so handles align mid-chest; keep shoulders down/back.

6) Shoulder Press (machine) 2 sets × 8–10 reps

Cue: pain-free range only; avoid shrugging.

7) Triceps Pushdown (cable) 2 sets × 10–12 reps

Cue: elbows stay near ribs; no swinging.

Upper Body Pull (back, biceps, posture)

8) Lat Pulldown 2–3 sets × 8–12 reps

Cue: pull to upper chest; do not pull behind the neck.

9) Seated Row 2 sets × 10–12 reps

Cue: squeeze shoulder blades together; keep ribs down.

10) Biceps Curl (machine or cable) 1–2 sets × 10–12 reps

Cue: slow reps; no shoulder rocking.

Core (stability, not sit-ups)

11) Plank (elevated if needed) 2 × 20–40 seconds

Cue: neutral spine; stop before form breaks.

OR: Dead Bug / Pallof Press 2 sets × 8–10 per side

Cue: brace gently; no lower-back arching.

Cool-Down (5 minutes)

  • Easy walking
  • Gentle stretching for hips, chest, and back

What to Avoid Initially

  • Heavy barbell squats or deadlifts
  • High-impact plyometrics
  • Training to failure
  • “Push through sharp pain” approaches

Expected Outcomes (if consistent)

  • 4–6 weeks: better balance, easier daily movement, reduced stiffness
  • 3–6 months: noticeable strength gains, better posture, improved muscle preservation, reduced fall risk

Practical rule: if you’re unsure about a machine setup, ask gym staff for a 60-second walkthrough before loading it.
