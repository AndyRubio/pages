Piano Practice Routines

30-Minute Routine (Tight, Focused)

1. Warm-up – 5 minutes

  • Slow scales (1–2 keys/day), hands separately → hands together.
  • One short technical pattern: broken chords or a Hanon/Firman exercise at a controlled tempo.

2. Technique & Foundation – 5 minutes

Pick one per session:

  • Arpeggios (major/minor of the day)
  • Contrary-motion scales
  • Simple chord progressions in multiple inversions

Focus on clean, even, relaxed playing.

3. Sight-Reading – 5 minutes

  • A fresh short piece every session (chorales, simple classical miniatures, easy études).
  • Do not stop for mistakes—maintain a steady tempo.

4. Repertoire (Core Pieces) – 12 minutes

  • 6 min: Isolate trouble spots (2–4 measure loops at slow tempo).
  • 6 min: Connect previously fixed sections; play at about 70–85% tempo.

Don’t waste time polishing what’s already solid.

5. Cool-down / Musicianship – 3 minutes

  • Play one piece you know well, gently and musically, or
  • Improvise quietly around a simple chord progression.

60-Minute Routine (Complete, Well-Rounded)

1. Warm-up – 8 minutes

  • Scales in 3–4 keys (major + relative minor).
  • One technical study: Hanon, Pischna, Dohnányi, or a Czerny excerpt.

Accuracy first; increase speed only when everything is even and controlled.

2. Technique & Drill Work – 10 minutes

Rotate through:

  • Arpeggios (all inversions).
  • Octave exercises.
  • Trills or finger-independence drills.
  • Voicing practice (melody louder than accompaniment).

Keep blocks short and concentrated rather than long and sloppy.

3. Sight-Reading – 7 minutes

  • One or two short pieces.
  • Keep your eyes ahead, recognize patterns, and don’t stop to fix errors mid-stream.
  • Use graded sight-reading books or simple classical repertoire.

4. Repertoire Work – 30 minutes

Break it down intelligently:

  • 10 min: Hardest sections → ultra-slow practice, rhythmic variation, hands separate as needed.
  • 10 min: Medium-difficulty passages → phrase shaping, articulation, dynamics.
  • 10 min: Run larger sections or the full piece; note where things fall apart.

Any weak spots you notice become the focus of the next session’s isolation work.

5. Cool-down / Ear & Creativity – 5 minutes

Pick one to finish:

  • Play something familiar at a slow, expressive tempo.
  • Improvise over a ii–V–I or another simple progression.
  • Do a quick ear drill (intervals or chord quality recognition at the keyboard).
