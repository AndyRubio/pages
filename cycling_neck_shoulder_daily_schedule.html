Daily Neck & Shoulder Pain-Prevention Schedule for Cycling

Built from your physio handouts. It assumes you are very unfit, so the starting dose is deliberately low. The target is consistency, not fatigue.

Daily base: 10–14 minStrength add-on: 2 days/weekCore add-on: 2 days/weekEasy effort only

Rules

Stop rule: stop any exercise that causes pain, pins and needles, numbness, dizziness, headache increase, or symptoms spreading into the arm. Do the next session with a smaller range or fewer reps.
Progress rule: only progress after 7 pain-free days. Add one small variable at a time: +1 rep, +5 seconds, or one extra set. Do not add all three.

The daily base routine

Upper-limb strength add-ons

Add-on A: neck + rotator cuff

  • Isometric neck flexion: up to 5 gentle presses, 3–5 seconds each.
  • Isometric neck extension: up to 5 gentle presses, 3–5 seconds each.
  • External rotation with band: 1 set of 5–8 reps each arm.
  • One-arm wall angel: 5 reps each side.

Add-on B: shoulder blade + cycling posture

  • External rotation with band: 1 set of 5–8 reps each arm.
  • Single-arm bent-over row: 1 set of 5–8 reps each arm, very light weight.
  • Step-up with arm elevation band: 1 set of 5 reps each leg.
  • Seated or standing wall angel: 5 controlled reps.

Core add-ons

Add-on A: basic stability

  • Front plank from knees or forearms/toes: 2 holds of 8–15 seconds.
  • Side plank from knees: 1 hold each side of 8–12 seconds.
  • Supine bridge: 1 set of 6 reps. Use both feet; add single-leg only later.

Add-on B: endurance, very low dose

  • Front plank: 2 holds of 10–20 seconds.
  • Supine bridge: 1 set of 6–8 reps.
  • Mountain climber: 1 slow set of 6 total knee drives, only if pain-free.

Skip boxer’s sit-up and Swiss-ball press-up hold for now unless your physio specifically told you to use them. They are harder than needed for a very unfit starting point.

Four-week progression

WeeksTargetDo not progress if
Week 1Learn the movements. Use the starting doses exactly.Any exercise causes pain or next-day neck/shoulder flare.
Week 2Add 1–2 reps to neck, wall angel, band, row, and bridge exercises.You cannot keep shoulders relaxed and breathing normal.
Week 3Increase posture holds by 5 seconds. Planks may reach 15–25 seconds.You compensate by shrugging, arching your back, or clenching the jaw.
Week 4Add a second set to band external rotation or rows only if everything is easy.Symptoms appear during cycling despite the lower-dose plan.

Two-minute pre-ride version

  1. 5 neck rotations each way.
  2. 5 chin tucks.
  3. 5 shoulder blade squeezes.
  4. 5 wall-angel reps or 5 arm circles each direction.
  5. Check cycling posture: relaxed shoulders, light hands, long neck, elbows soft.

When to reduce the plan

Use only the daily base items 1–6 for 3–5 days if you are sore, sleep-deprived, returning after illness, or your cycling volume increases. Resume add-ons only after symptoms settle.

Source handouts used: Neck and shoulder mobility; Neck and upper limb strengthening; Stretching for cycling; Core strength for cycling. The physio handouts also state to stop any exercise that causes pain and to consult the therapist if discomfort occurs.
